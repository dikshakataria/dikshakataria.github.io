Operation Streamline

Operation Streamline is a joint initiative of the Department of Homeland Security and Department of Justice in the United States , started in 2005, that adopts a "zero-tolerance" approach to unauthorized border-crossing by engaging in criminal prosecution of those engaging in it. Up to 70 people are tried at the same time, sometimes wearing shackles in the courtroom. Entering without inspection is a misdemeanor, and re-entering after deportation is a felony. [1] [2] [3] [4]
