Refugees of the Syrian Civil War , widely referred as the Syrian refugees , [25] are Syrian nationals, who have fled Syria with the escalation of the Syrian Civil War . [26] To escape the violence, roughly two and a half million Syrian refugees have fled the country to neighboring Jordan , [27] Lebanon , Turkey , [28] [29] Iraq and Iraqi Kurdistan , [30] while thousands also ended up in more distant countries of the Caucasus, the Persian Gulf and North Africa.

Refugees of the Syrian civil war or Syrian refugees [1] [ verification needed ] are Syrian nationals, who have fled Syria with the escalation of the Syrian civil war . To escape the violence, tens of thousands of Syrian refugees have fled the country to neighboring Jordan , [4] Lebanon , Turkey , [5] [6] Iraq and Iraqi Kurdistan . [7] No Syrian refugees have yet arrived at the Israeli border. [8] In August 2012, the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR) reported that the number of registered Syrian refugees had reached over 200,000, exceeding the UNHCR estimate of 185,000 for the entire year. [9] Also according to the United Nations, 2.5 million people inside Syria needed help and about 1.5 million Syrians were internally displaced because of the Syrian civil war. [9] [10]
