Gangs in the United States

Gangs in the United States include several types of groups, including national street gangs , local street gangs, prison gangs , motorcycle clubs , and ethnic and organized crime gangs. [3] Approximately 1.4 million people were part of gangs as of 2011, and more than 33,000 gangs were active in the United States. [4]

Street gangs in the United States have a long and complex history dating to the early 19th century. [2] An exception was noted in 1853 Philadelphia. While kids from more affluent neighborhoods may turn to other less dangerous alternatives, children from poorer neighborhoods often turn to gangs both as protection and a place to find love and understanding.
